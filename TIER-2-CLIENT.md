# TIER 2: Client-Facing (INT Clients)

## What is Tier 2?
Playbook for INT clients who are onboarding or executing with the Customization Service.

**Use case:** "Here's how to work with us. Here's what success looks like. Here's how to execute."

**Audience:** INT clients, prospects, INT sales when sharing with prospects

---

## Branch Structure
```
vistadel-complete/ (main)
├── Phase content (public, lite version)
├── case-studies/ (lite: anonymized or INT-approved)
└── TIER-2-CLIENT.md (this file)
```

---

## What's Included in Tier 2

### 1. All 8 Phases (Client-Focused Version)
- **Core content:** Same as Tier 1, reframed for client perspective
- **Examples:** Same case studies, but anonymized (e.g., "Law Firm" instead of "Martensen") OR INT-approved named clients
- **Your role vs. INT's role:** Clear responsibility matrix for each phase
- **Success criteria:** What does Phase completion look like? When can we move to next phase?
- **Timeline expectations:** How long does each phase take? What blocks timeline?

### 2. Fillable Templates (Google Docs)
- Problem Definition worksheet
- User Persona templates
- Data Inventory spreadsheet
- Stack Scorecard
- Test Plan template
- Deployment Checklist
- Roadmap prioritization framework
- Launch metrics dashboard

### 3. FAQ (Client Version)
- "How long will this take?"
- "What's included in each phase?"
- "What happens if we discover new requirements mid-project?"
- "How do we know we're ready for the next phase?"
- "What's your success rate?"
- "How do you handle scope creep?"

### 4. Success Metrics (Client-Facing)
- Phase completion signals
- Quality gates per phase
- Onboarding timeline
- Expected outcomes by phase

### 5. Support Resources
- Slack/email support contacts
- Office hours schedule
- Escalation process
- Change request process

### 6. Glossary
- Client-friendly explanations of technical terms
- "What is RLS?" "What is CI/CD?" "What's a webhook?"

---

## What's NOT in Tier 2

- Internal INT processes/team structure
- Pricing or cost models (that's Tier 1)
- Profit margins or financial terms
- Team headcount or resource allocation
- Internal risk assessments
- Competitive positioning (keep this natural, not defensive)

---

## Tone (Client Tier)

**Goal:** Professional but accessible, confident but not arrogant, supportive without hand-holding.

**Examples:**
- ❌ "We'll handle all the complexity for you (you're not smart enough)"
- ✅ "We'll guide you through complexity and explain each decision"
- ❌ "This is industry standard"
- ✅ "This is industry standard, and here's why it matters for your business"
- ❌ "Trust us, it works"
- ✅ "Here's how other companies in your industry solved this"

---

## Access Control

**Who has access:**
- Current INT clients
- Qualified prospects (shared by sales)
- INT team (for sharing)

**How:** Shared Google Docs + gated Notion workspace (comment access for feedback)

---

## Key Differences from Tier 1

| Aspect | Tier 1 (Internal) | Tier 2 (Client) |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Tone** | Direct, technical, honest | Professional, accessible, supportive |
| **Examples** | Real client names (Martensen, etc.) | Anonymized or approved names |
| **Case studies** | Full implementation details | What client can learn |
| **Cost/pricing** | Full cost breakdown | Reference to "services agreement" |
| **Timeline** | Realistic (with risk buffers) | Conservative (under-promise) |
| **Liability** | Lists INT's responsibilities | Lists mutual responsibilities |
| **Metrics** | INT's success metrics | Client's success metrics |

---

## Updating Tier 2

When Tier 1/3 content changes:
1. Update client-facing content
2. Use client-friendly language
3. Remove pricing/internal details
4. Review for tone (supportive, not patronizing)
5. Get client feedback via Notion comments
